# Week 1 Self-Assessment

## 1. What is a stock? Explain in 3 sentences.

A stock represents ownership in a company. When a person buys a stock, they become a shareholder and own a small portion of that business. Investors buy stocks with the expectation that the company's value will increase over time, allowing them to earn returns.

---

## 2. What does the "Close" price represent in stock data?

The Close price is the final price at which a stock was traded when the market closes for the day. It is one of the most commonly used values in stock analysis because it reflects the market's final valuation of the stock for that trading session. Many technical indicators are calculated using the Close price.

---

## 3. What's the difference between "Close" and "Adj Close"?

The Close price is the stock's actual closing market price on a particular day. The Adjusted Close (Adj Close) modifies the Close price to account for corporate actions such as dividends, stock splits, and bonus issues. Adjusted Close provides a more accurate picture of the stock's historical performance over time.

---

## 4. What is a moving average, and why is it useful?

A moving average is the average stock price over a specific number of previous trading days. It helps smooth out daily price fluctuations and makes trends easier to identify. Investors use moving averages to understand the overall direction of a stock and reduce the impact of short-term market noise.

---

## 5. Why might a stock's 50-day MA crossing its 200-day MA be a significant event?

The 50-day moving average represents the short-term trend, while the 200-day moving average represents the long-term trend. When the 50-day moving average crosses above the 200-day moving average, it is often viewed as a bullish signal called a Golden Cross. When it crosses below the 200-day moving average, it is often viewed as a bearish signal called a Death Cross.
