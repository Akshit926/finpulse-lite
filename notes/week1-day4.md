### Critical Thinking Question

#### What does a moving average actually tell you?

A moving average shows the average stock price over a specific period of time. Instead of focusing on daily fluctuations, it helps reveal the overall direction or trend of the stock. It smooths out short-term noise and makes it easier to understand whether a stock is generally moving upward, downward, or sideways.

#### How does it differ from the actual price?

The actual stock price represents the market value at a specific moment and can change significantly from day to day. A moving average is calculated using many previous prices, so it changes more gradually. While the actual price reflects immediate market activity, the moving average reflects the broader trend.

#### Why might investors care about a moving average crossing another moving average?

When a short-term moving average (such as the 50-day moving average) crosses above a long-term moving average (such as the 200-day moving average), it may indicate that momentum is becoming positive and the stock could be entering an upward trend. This is often called a "Golden Cross."

When the short-term moving average crosses below the long-term moving average, it may indicate weakening momentum and a potential downward trend. This is often called a "Death Cross."

Investors monitor these crossovers because they can provide signals about changes in market sentiment and long-term trends.
